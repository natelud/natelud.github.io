Autonomous agents in human environments must collaborate with people whose intentions are implicit and never directly communicated. We formulate ad-hoc human-robot teaming as a POMDP where the human policy is an unobservable latent variable, and learn joint human-robot strategy representations from human-human demonstrations using a VAE. An LSTM-based belief tracker infers the partner's latent strategy online from partial trajectories, and an MPPI controller selects robot actions by scoring candidate trajectory pairs decoded from sampled joint strategy embeddings. Evaluated on Overcooked-AI, our method outperforms PPO with Fictitious Co-Play and Behavioral Cloning baselines by 1.2-2.2x across non-adaptive partner scenarios, and by 47-72% under adaptive partner conditions.
