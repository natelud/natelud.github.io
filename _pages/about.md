I am a robotics software engineer who started in mechanical design and grew into full-stack autonomy. Over two years in industry I built perception, planning, and control software for industrial robot arms, including a domain-specific language that let LLMs program manipulators from natural language. Across four years of research I have published in reinforcement learning, autonomous driving, and physical human-robot collaboration, and I now want to bring that range to a team shipping real-world robotics.

I'm finishing an M.S. in Robotics at [Carnegie Mellon University](https://www.ri.cmu.edu/) (July 2026), advised by [Prof. Katia Sycara](https://www.ri.cmu.edu/ri-faculty/katia-sycara/) in the [AART Lab](https://www.ri.cmu.edu/robotics-groups/advanced-agent-robotics-technology-lab/). I'm open to **robotics SWE and ML/RL engineering roles starting Summer 2026** — [download my CV (PDF)]({{ site.baseurl }}/files/CV_Nate_Ludlow.pdf).

## What I Work On

- **Robotics software & systems** — ROS / ROS 2 stacks, manipulation pipelines, perception → planning → control, real-time MPC, and shipping code on real hardware
- **Reinforcement & imitation learning** — multi-agent RL, behavioral cloning from human demos, world models, neuro-symbolic / knowledge-graph-augmented policies
- **Safety-critical autonomy** — Control Barrier Functions, Hamilton-Jacobi reachability, latent safety filters, risk-aware planning under uncertainty
- **Perception** — SPAD LiDAR, image segmentation for industrial robots, classical and deep computer vision

## Selected Highlights

## Background

Before CMU I earned a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at BYU (Dynamic Systems, Controls & Robotics; minors in CS and Math). I spent three years in the [RAD Lab](http://radlab.byu.edu/) under [Prof. Marc Killpack](https://www.me.byu.edu/directory/marc-killpack) building omnidirectional mobile bases, impedance-controlled co-manipulation, and a VR experimental platform for human-robot interaction studies. I led the BYU Crop Biomechanics capstone team that built an automated maize-stalk stiffness sensor — published in MDPI Sensors 2025 — and worked at [Altitude AI](https://altitudeai.com/) on perception, planning, and control for industrial meat-processing robots.

At CMU I completed the [Robotics Institute Summer Scholars (RISS) Fellowship](https://riss.ri.cmu.edu/student/nathan-ludlow/) in the DRIVE Lab on human driver modeling for autonomous vehicles (ICRA 2024), then helped develop CBF-inspired Voronoi navigation for safe multi-agent control. I now work in the AART Lab on neuro-symbolic methods for collaborative robotics.
